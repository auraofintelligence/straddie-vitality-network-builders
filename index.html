Evidence Leads The Build

The project starts with mechanisms, source dates and practical safety design. Evidence works best when it is clear enough for residents, venues, sponsors and governments to act carefully.

Oxygen pressure

HBOT is pressure physiology

HBOT/PADS is about pressure increasing dissolved oxygen in plasma, like gas held in sparkling water under pressure. The useful questions are dose, supervision, tissue need, sensor logging and safety.

Heat and skin

Skin is a two-way filter

Heat opens skin blood vessels, raises heart rate and makes sweat cool the body. The skin also controls inward and outward molecular flow, so barrier care, hydration, minerals and clean exposure matter.

Cellular repair

Fasting is a spectrum

Time-restricted eating, added-sugar pauses, fasting-mimicking cycles and supervised 2-5 day fasts belong on different lanes. The deeper the fast, the more screening, electrolytes, medication review and refeeding planning matter.

Toxin effects

Hormones and inflammation matter

PFAS, heavy metals and endocrine-disrupting chemicals belong in a practical exposure story because they can intersect with hormones, fertility, inflammation, metabolism, energy and cognition.

Repair markers

Telomeres and stem cells belong

HBOT research includes telomere, senescent-cell and stem/progenitor-cell signals. These are research-grade evidence trails for careful measurement and review.

System savings

Small gains can mean billions

Australia spent $270.5 billion on health in 2023-24. One cent saved in every dollar is about $2.7 billion a year, so prevention, cleaner data, fewer repeated forms and earlier support are serious public questions.

Local proof

Straddie can test the stack

Venues can collect attendance, dose, safety, access, satisfaction, recovery, source-note and public/private data so the project learns in public with staged evidence.

Signal lab

Self-experiments can stay private

People and venues can test safe routines, share consented summaries, keep raw data local and turn subtle ideas into logged variables, repeat tests and source-dated Markdown notes.
