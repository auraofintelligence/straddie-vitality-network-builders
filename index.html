Evidence Leads The Build

The project starts with mechanisms, source dates and practical safety design. Evidence does not need hype; it needs to be clear enough for residents, venues, sponsors and governments to act carefully.

Oxygen pressure

HBOT is pressure physiology

HBOT/PADS is about pressure increasing dissolved oxygen in plasma, like gas held in sparkling water under pressure. The useful questions are dose, supervision, tissue need, sensor logging and safety.

Heat and skin

Sauna is controlled heat stress

Heat opens skin blood vessels, raises heart rate and makes sweat cool the body. That can support practical routines, but hydration, contraindications and personal tolerance decide whether it is helpful.

Detox boundary

Detox is plumbing, not magic

The stronger line is exposure reduction, liver processing, kidney filtering, gut exit routes, hydration and careful measurement. Sweat can carry some substances, but it is mainly a cooling system.

Toxin effects

Hormones and inflammation matter

PFAS, heavy metals and endocrine-disrupting chemicals belong in a practical exposure story because they can intersect with hormones, fertility, inflammation, metabolism, energy and cognition.

System savings

Small gains can mean billions

Australia spent $270.5 billion on health in 2023-24. One cent saved in every dollar is about $2.7 billion a year, so prevention, cleaner data, fewer repeated forms and earlier support are serious public questions.

Local proof

Straddie can test the stack

Venues can collect attendance, dose, safety, access, satisfaction, recovery, source-note and public/private data so the project learns in public without pretending it has all answers on day one.
