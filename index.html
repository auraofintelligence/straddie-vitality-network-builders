Evidence Leads The Build

The project starts with public health need, source dates and practical safety design. Evidence does not need hype; it needs to be clear enough for residents, venues, sponsors and governments to act carefully.

Public health need

Dementia is already here

AIHW reports 443,274 Australians living with dementia in 2025 and dementia as Australia's leading cause of death in 2023. That supports serious prevention, care, access and local wellbeing planning now.

Repair stack

HBOT/PADS needs supervision

The preferred path is mask-fed PADS in an air-pressurised chamber, with adjustable gas mix and flow, biosensor logging, clear fire controls and qualified review before any public claim.

Everyday wellbeing

Sauna has a real evidence trail

Sauna research includes strong observational cardiovascular associations and practical athlete heat-adaptation studies. It is useful, but public copy must not turn association into cure language.

Prevention

Risk factors are modifiable

AIHW reports a substantial share of Australia's disease burden is linked to modifiable risk factors, which supports food, movement, social connection and safer environment work.

Exposure context

Toxicity needs precise wording

PFAS exposure is widespread. The public line should be exposure reduction, safer choices and source trails, not broad detox promises that outrun the evidence.

Local proof

Straddie can gather better data

Venues can collect attendance, safety, access, satisfaction, recovery and source-note data so the project learns in public without pretending it has all answers on day one.
